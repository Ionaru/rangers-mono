---
title: Formations
---

The formations below are for a squad, but the same shapes are used at platoon
level. Each one trades firepower, security, concealment and manoeuvrability
differently, so pick the one that fits the ground and the threat.

## Wedge

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/Wedge.png" alt="Wedge formation">

**Pros**

- Good situational awareness
- Flanks are secured
- Good firepower towards the front
- Good flexibility to change to other formations

**Cons**

- Concealment is limited; wide squad

**Use when**

- It is the default squad formation
- You expect hostiles from a ~180° arc in front of you
- You are in the centre of a platoon wedge

## Staggered column

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/StaggeredColumn.png" alt="Staggered column formation">

**Pros**

- Easy to establish
- Very good flank security
- Good concealment
- Good flexibility to change to other formations
- Good for crossing difficult terrain

**Cons**

- Bad firepower to the front

**Use when**

- In a platoon staggered column
- Moving along a road while not expecting hostiles
- In limited terrain

## Line

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/Line.png" alt="Line formation">

**Pros**

- Maximum firepower to the front
- Ideal for popping up on ridge lines in ambush scenarios

**Cons**

- Bad firepower to the flanks
- Limited manoeuvrability

**Use when**

- Attacking hostiles in front of you while not expecting hostiles on the flanks
- Climbing an elevation line to conduct an assault with hostiles in front
- In a platoon line

## File

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/Column.png" alt="File formation">

**Pros**

- Easy to establish
- Good flank security
- Very good concealment
- Good for crossing difficult terrain

**Cons**

- Very bad firepower to the front
- Bad manoeuvrability

**Use when**

- In very limited terrain
- Moving through minefields

## Echelon (right)

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/EchelonRight.png" alt="Echelon right formation">

**Pros**

- Good firepower to the front
- Flank security to one side
- Can switch to line very easily

**Cons**

- Limited manoeuvrability to one flank (here: left)

**Use when**

- Intending to switch to line soon
- Moving in a platoon wedge

## Diamond

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/Diamond.png" alt="Diamond formation">

**Pros**

- Excellent all-round (360°) security
- Good firepower in every direction, including the rear
- Strong when the threat can come from any bearing

**Cons**

- Spread out, so concealment is limited
- Needs space and is harder to hold in tight terrain
- Less concentrated firepower to the front than a wedge or line

**Use when**

- You expect contact from any direction, including the rear
- In the combat area or close quarters with an all-round threat
- The threat bearing is unclear

## Wedge on wedge

<img class="transparent-fix" src="/wiki/images/WedgeOnWedge.png" alt="Wedge-on-wedge squad formation">

A squad column with both fire teams in a wedge, and the default squad movement
formation. The lead fire team moves in a wedge, the trail fire team follows in
its own wedge, and the squad leader (with the medic) sits between them: far
enough forward to see the lead team and react to contact, far enough back to
avoid being caught in the first exchange of fire. The lead team is most likely
to make first contact; the trail team is the squad leader's manoeuvre element
once it does.

**Pros**

- Good all-round security: the two wedges cover the front, both flanks and the rear
- Good dispersion, while staying close enough for voice and hand-signal control
- Strong firepower to the front and the flanks
- The lead team can return fire immediately without masking the trail team
- Flexible: collapses into a line to assault, or a file for tight terrain

**Cons**

- Wide and spread out, so concealment is limited
- Not the fastest formation through dense terrain
- Less firepower to the front than a line

**Use when**

- It is the default squad movement formation
- Contact is possible but you still need to cover ground
- You want front, flank and rear security with the leader kept covered
